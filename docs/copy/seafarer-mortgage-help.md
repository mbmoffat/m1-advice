## Opening

Yes, seafarers can and do get UK mortgages. Whether you can comes down to how your income is evidenced and finding a lender that fits how you are paid. The hurdles are well known: foreign currency pay, the Seafarers Earnings Deduction, long spells at sea and residency. None of them is a barrier with a lender that understands seafarers, and that is what specialist help gets you.

## Reassurance — Whatever your situation at sea, it is workable

Whatever your situation at sea, it is workable.

Seafarer income rarely fits a standard payslip template. That is presentation, not a problem. We regularly place clients who are:

- Paid in a foreign currency (USD, EUR or other)
- Working rotational or voyage-based contracts
- Claiming Seafarers' Earnings Deduction (SED)
- Spending long stretches outside the UK
- Merchant navy, yacht, cruise or offshore crew
- Mixing salary, allotments and overtime

If your circumstances are not listed, they are almost certainly still workable. The 60-second check is the quickest way to find out.

## What makes a seafarer mortgage different?

On paper a seafarer is a strong borrower with steady, contracted income. The trouble is that an automated high street check is built around PAYE payslips and a settled UK footprint, so foreign currency, a nil tax bill and time at sea can each trip it. Specialist help means taking your case to a lender that reads those features as normal, because for this line of work they are.

## Foreign currency pay

If you are paid in US dollars or euros, a lender converts the income to sterling and applies a reduction to allow for the rate moving, then works from that figure. The size of the reduction varies, so the lender you choose matters more than the rate. Links to the foreign currency income page (`/seafarer-mortgage-foreign-currency-income`).

## The Seafarers Earnings Deduction

The Seafarers Earnings Deduction (SED) is a relief from HM Revenue and Customs that removes UK income tax on qualifying earnings for work carried out outside the UK, claimed over an eligible period of at least 365 days according to HM Revenue and Customs. It is legitimate and well established, but it makes your taxable income look low, which an automated check can misread. The right lender works from your gross contractual pay instead. Links to the Seafarers Earnings Deduction page (`/seafarers-earnings-deduction-mortgage`).

## Time at sea, rotation and merchant navy pay

Rotational contracts and long stretches away are routine, and a lender that knows the sector treats a settled rotation as stable employment rather than a gap. Merchant navy crews often combine all of these at once, which we cover on our merchant navy mortgages page (`/merchant-navy-mortgage`).

## FAQ

1. **Do I need a specialist broker for a seafarer mortgage?** It helps a great deal. Seafarer income is read very differently from one lender to the next, and a broker who places these cases regularly knows which lenders treat your pay fairly. The work is in matching your circumstances to the right lender, not in the eligibility itself.
2. **Will the Seafarers Earnings Deduction stop me getting a mortgage?** No. The deduction lowers your UK tax, not your earnings. A lender that understands seafarers works from your gross contractual pay rather than the low tax figure on your return, so the relief is not the obstacle. Lender choice is.
